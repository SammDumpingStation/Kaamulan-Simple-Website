Discover Kaamulan and Exploring its Origin

Discover the Kaamulan Festival: its history, evolution, and significance. Experience the vibrant celebration and learn about the traditions and community that make it special.

Its roots and origin

The Kaamulan Festival has its roots in the indigenous cultures of the province of Bukidnon, Philippines. It traces its origins to pre-colonial times when the indigenous tribes of Bukidnon held rituals and gatherings to celebrate bountiful harvests, important milestones, and significant events in their communities.

The festival's modern form can be attributed to the efforts of the late Benedicto T. Caballero, who served as the first governor of Bukidnon. In 1974, Caballero conceptualized and organized the Kaamulan Festival as a way to unite the various indigenous tribes in the province and showcase their rich cultural heritage.

The festival aimed to foster understanding, appreciation, and respect for the indigenous traditions and customs that had been an integral part of Bukidnon's history for centuries. It provided a platform for the tribes to come together, exchange cultural practices, and strengthen their collective identity.

Where is it celebrated?

The Kaamulan Festival is celebrated annually in the province of Bukidnon, located on the island of Mindanao, in the Philippines. The festival takes place in the capital city of Malaybalay, which serves as the main venue for the festivities.

The exact dates of the Kaamulan Festival can vary from year to year, but it is typically held during the last week of February until the second week of April. It is a multi-day celebration, spanning several days of vibrant activities, cultural showcases, and events.

During the festival, the streets of Malaybalay come alive with colorful parades, street dancing competitions, beauty pageants, and other cultural performances. Traditional music, dances, and rituals are showcased, and visitors can experience the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous tribes of Bukidnon.
